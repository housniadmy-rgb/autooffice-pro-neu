# 09 — Legal & Transfer

## Purpose

Legal paperwork and asset-transfer mechanics. Answers *"How do I actually get ownership, and what am I signing?"*

## Files that will be added

- `mutual-nda-template.pdf` — standard mutual NDA the buyer signs before accessing NDA-tier folders
- `asset-purchase-agreement-template.docx` — draft APA covering: source code IP transfer, domain assignment, brand assets, 30-day support terms, escrow release conditions, buyer's ongoing hosting responsibility
- `ip-declaration.md` — seller's declaration of original authorship, no third-party proprietary code, all dependencies permissively licensed
- `transfer-checklist.md` — step-by-step: GitHub → domain → Cloudflare → Render → Brevo → 30-day support handoff (mirrors `sale/Transfer_Checklist.md` with any DD-specific updates)
- `escrow-terms.md` — Flippa Escrow process, funds release conditions, dispute resolution
- `warranties-and-disclaimers.md` — what the seller warrants (authorship, non-infringement) and what is sold "as-is" (no security audit, no HIPAA, no PMF)
- `credentials-transfer-log.md` — form the buyer signs when each credential set is transferred (GitHub, domain, Render, etc.) — created empty, filled in during actual transfer

## NDA classification

**Confidential.** This folder unlocks only under a Letter of Intent or once escrow is active. Templates (NDA and APA) may be shared at NDA stage. Filled-in credentials transfer logs are never distributed — they exist for record-keeping between seller and specific buyer.

## Reader notes

- Templates are seller-provided; buyer is expected to have their own attorney review and mark up before signature.
- The seller does not provide legal advice. All templates are structured for a common asset-sale scenario but must be adapted to the buyer's jurisdiction.
- Trademark ownership is explicitly not part of the sale — the "PraxisOnline24" mark is unregistered, and the buyer is free to file in their target market.
